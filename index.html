How This Works — Rounds Explained

Each workout is 3 rounds of the same exercises — you do all the exercises in the list once, that's round 1. Then rest 60 seconds and repeat the exact same exercises for round 2, then round 3. You're not picking different exercises each round. Start at the lower rep count listed and add reps week by week as it gets easier. You should feel challenged but not destroyed — if you can't finish a set, drop the reps. If it's too easy, add reps or slow down the movement. Takes 20–30 min per session.

Push Day — Chest, Shoulders & Triceps

3 rounds. Rest 60 sec between rounds. ~20 minutes total.

1. Standard Pushups

  • Reps: 3 × 10–15
  • How: Hands shoulder-width apart, body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower chest to just above the floor, push back up. Don't let hips sag or shoot up.
  • Too hard? Do them on your knees until you build strength
  • Too easy? Slow down the lowering phase — 3 seconds down, 1 second up
  • Build to: 3 × 20 reps, then add slow negatives or try decline pushups (feet elevated)

2. Pike Pushups

  • Reps: 3 × 8–12
  • How: Start in a downward dog position (hips high, body in an inverted V). Bend elbows and lower your head toward the floor, then push back up. Hits shoulders hard.
  • Feel it in: Front and side deltoids
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps, then try elevating your feet on a chair for more shoulder load

3. Tricep Dips (Chair)

  • Reps: 3 × 10–12
  • How: Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair, hands gripping the edge beside your hips. Slide off the seat and lower yourself down by bending your elbows to 90°, then push back up. Keep your back close to the chair.
  • Feel it in: Back of your arms (triceps)
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps, then try straightening your legs out further to increase the load

4. Shoulder Taps

  • Reps: 3 × 20 taps (10 each side)
  • How: Get into a pushup position. Without rotating your hips, lift one hand and tap the opposite shoulder, then alternate. Keep your core tight — the goal is to minimize side-to-side movement.
  • Feel it in: Shoulders, core stability
  • Build to: 3 × 30 taps with zero hip rotation

5. Plank Hold

  • Time: 3 × 30–45 seconds
  • How: Forearms on the floor, body straight from head to heels. Squeeze your core like you're bracing for a punch. Don't let your lower back arch or hips drop.
  • Build to: 60 seconds per set over 4–6 weeks, then try side planks

Pull Day — Back & Biceps

3 rounds. Rest 60 sec between rounds. ~20 minutes total. Pull days are harder without equipment — these are your best no-equipment options.

1. Doorframe Rows

  • Reps: 3 × 8–12
  • How: Stand in a doorframe and grip both sides at about chest height. Lean back so your body is at an angle (like a reverse pushup), arms extended. Pull your chest toward the doorframe by squeezing your shoulder blades together, then slowly lower back. The more you lean back, the harder it is.
  • Feel it in: Upper back, rear shoulders, biceps
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps, then lean back further to increase the angle and difficulty

2. Superman Holds

  • Reps: 3 × 10–12, hold 2 sec at top
  • How: Lie face down on the floor, arms extended in front of you. Simultaneously lift your arms, chest, and legs off the ground as high as you can. Hold for 2 seconds, lower slowly. Don't use momentum — squeeze your back muscles.
  • Feel it in: Lower back, rear shoulders, glutes
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps with a 3-second hold at the top

3. Reverse Snow Angels

  • Reps: 3 × 12–15
  • How: Lie face down, arms at your sides. Lift your arms off the ground and sweep them up overhead (like a snow angel but in reverse and off the ground). Keep arms straight and off the floor the whole time.
  • Feel it in: Upper and mid back, rear deltoids
  • Build to: 3 × 20 reps while holding each end position for 1 second

4. Towel / Backpack Curls

  • Reps: 3 × 10–15
  • How: Fill a backpack with books or water bottles for weight. Hold the straps and do a standard bicep curl motion. Or loop a towel under a door and grip both ends to do a pull motion. Not perfect, but it works.
  • Feel it in: Biceps
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps, then add more weight to the backpack

5. Dead Bug

  • Reps: 3 × 10 each side
  • How: Lie on your back, arms straight up, knees at 90° in the air. Slowly lower one arm overhead while extending the opposite leg toward the floor — don't let your back arch. Return and switch sides. Looks easy, isn't.
  • Feel it in: Deep core, coordination
  • Build to: 3 × 15 each side with a slower, more controlled movement

Legs Day — Quads, Glutes & Calves

3 rounds. Rest 60 sec between rounds. ~20 minutes. Leg workouts are brutal with bodyweight — don't underestimate these.

1. Bodyweight Squats

  • Reps: 3 × 15–20
  • How: Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out. Send hips back and down like you're sitting into a chair. Keep your chest up, knees tracking over your toes. Go until thighs are parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable), then drive through your heels back up.
  • Feel it in: Quads, glutes, hamstrings
  • Build to: 3 × 25 reps, then try jump squats or slow 4-second descent squats

2. Reverse Lunges

  • Reps: 3 × 10 each leg
  • How: Stand tall. Step one foot back and lower your back knee toward the floor (don't slam it). Front knee stays at 90°, chest up. Push through your front heel to return. Reverse lunges are easier on the knees than forward lunges.
  • Feel it in: Quads, glutes, balance
  • Build to: 3 × 15 each leg, then try walking lunges or holding something heavy

3. Glute Bridges

  • Reps: 3 × 15–20, hold 1 sec at top
  • How: Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on floor hip-width apart. Drive through your heels to lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze your glutes hard at the top, then lower slowly.
  • Feel it in: Glutes, hamstrings, lower back
  • Build to: 3 × 25 reps, then try single-leg glute bridges (one leg extended)

4. Wall Sit

  • Time: 3 × 30–45 seconds
  • How: Back flat against a wall, slide down until thighs are parallel to the floor (like sitting in an invisible chair). Hold. Arms can rest on thighs or extend forward. It will burn — that's normal.
  • Build to: 60 seconds per set, then 90 seconds

5. Calf Raises

  • Reps: 3 × 20–25
  • How: Stand with feet hip-width apart. Rise up onto your toes as high as you can, hold 1 second at the top, then lower slowly. Do these on a step edge (heel hanging off) for greater range of motion if available.
  • Feel it in: Calves
  • Build to: 3 × 30 reps, then try single-leg calf raises

Cardio + Core Day

2–3 rounds. Minimal rest between exercises (20–30 sec), 90 sec rest between rounds. Gets your heart rate up and builds core strength at the same time. ~20 minutes.

1. High Knees

  • Time: 3 × 30 seconds
  • How: Run in place driving your knees up to hip height as fast as you can. Pump your arms. Land lightly on the balls of your feet. This is your warm-up and cardio rolled into one.
  • Build to: 3 × 45 seconds at full pace

2. Mountain Climbers

  • Reps: 3 × 20 (10 each leg)
  • How: Start in a pushup position. Drive one knee toward your chest, then quickly switch legs like you're running horizontally. Keep your hips level — don't let them bounce up. The faster you go, the more cardio it becomes.
  • Feel it in: Core, hip flexors, shoulders
  • Build to: 3 × 30 reps at a faster pace

3. Crunches

  • Reps: 3 × 15–20
  • How: Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Hands behind your head (don't pull your neck). Curl your shoulders off the floor by contracting your abs — you don't need to come all the way up. Lower slowly. The lowering is where most of the work happens.
  • Build to: 3 × 25 reps, then try bicycle crunches for more difficulty

4. Leg Raises

  • Reps: 3 × 10–12
  • How: Lie flat on your back, legs straight. Press your lower back into the floor. Slowly raise both legs to 90°, then lower them back down without letting them touch the floor. If your back arches, bend your knees slightly.
  • Feel it in: Lower abs, hip flexors
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps with fully straight legs the whole way

5. Burpees

  • Reps: 3 × 8–10
  • How: Stand → squat down and place hands on floor → jump or step feet back into a pushup position → do one pushup → jump or step feet forward → jump up with arms overhead. That's one rep. It's hard. Do them at whatever pace you can maintain.
  • This is your finisher — do these last in the circuit
  • Build to: 3 × 15 reps at a consistent pace without stopping
No Jump Alternative

If you're working out late at night and don't want to make noise, swap high knees for slow mountain climbers, and swap burpees for a plank + squat combo. Same workout, just quieter.

How to Shave — Step by Step
PREP

Soften the Hair First

Shave after your shower — the warm water and steam soften the hair follicles and makes the razor glide easier. Never shave on completely dry skin.

APPLY

Apply Shave Cream

A pea-sized amount of shave cream, worked into the area with your fingers. Let it sit for 30 seconds before you start.

SHAVE

Short Strokes, With the Grain

Always shave with the grain (the direction the hair grows). Use short, light strokes — don't press hard. Let the razor do the work. Rinse the blade after every 2–3 strokes.

AVOID

Don't Go Against the Grain (yet)

Going against the grain gives a closer shave but dramatically increases razor burn and ingrown hairs — especially bad with eczema. Master with-the-grain first.

RINSE

Rinse with Cold Water

Cold water closes the pores and calms the skin after shaving. Pat completely dry with a clean towel.

AFTER

Apply Aftershave Balm — Then Moisturizer

Pat a small amount of Nivea Sensitive balm over the shaved areas. Let it absorb a minute, then apply your regular face moisturizer over everything.

BLADE

Replace Blades Regularly

With light facial hair, a cartridge should last 4–6 shaves. If it starts pulling instead of gliding, swap it.

How Often to Shave

With mostly upper lip and chin growth right now, you probably only need to shave every 3–5 days. Don't shave every day — your skin needs time to recover, especially with eczema.
